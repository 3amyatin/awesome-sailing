# Netherlands

The Netherlands is unusually strong for practical cruising research because official waterway, tide, bridge, and Wadden references are easy to combine into one planning flow.

## Overview
- Use this page to move from official Dutch water-information sources into Wadden, IJsselmeer, and inland-waterway trip planning.
- The strongest references here are water-level, tide, and route-planning tools rather than general tourism sites.

## Official information
- [Netherlands Coast Guard help](https://kustwacht.nl/en/help/) - Official emergency and help entry point for Dutch waters. 🇳🇱

## Ports and marinas
- [Waddenhavens](https://waddenhavens.nl/en/) - Practical English gateway to marinas and ports in the Dutch Wadden area. 🇳🇱
- [Varen Doe Je Samen](https://www.varendoejesamen.nl/) - Practical Dutch boating knowledge base that helps when narrowing down routes and local waterway decisions. 🇳🇱

## Weather
- [Rijkswaterstaat water reports](https://waterberichtgeving.rws.nl/) - Official Dutch water-level and waterway-information portal. 🇳🇱
- [Rijkswaterstaat tides](https://getij.rws.nl/) - Official tide and water-level reference. 🇳🇱

## Rules and paperwork
- [Netherlands Coast Guard preparation for water sports](https://kustwacht.nl/watersporters/voorbereiding/) - Official preparation guidance for recreational boaters. 🇳🇱
- [Varen Doe Je Samen](https://www.varendoejesamen.nl/) - Good practical follow-up for bridges, locks, etiquette, and route-specific boating knowledge. 🇳🇱
