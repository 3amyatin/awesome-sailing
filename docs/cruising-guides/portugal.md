# Portugal

Portugal is an Atlantic-first cruising guide with official marine forecasts, hydrographic references, and the port authorities that matter most on the mainland, Madeira, and the Azores.

## Overview
- Use this page as a practical starting point for Portugal's Atlantic coast, Madeira, and Azores planning.
- The strongest references here are official weather, oceanographic forecasts, and island-port authorities.

## Official information
- [IPMA maritime bulletins](https://www.ipma.pt/pt/maritima/boletins/) - Official Portuguese marine bulletins and forecasts. 🇵🇹
- [Portuguese Hydrographic Institute meteo-oceanographic forecasts](https://www.hidrografico.pt/en/generic-pages/dt/dcdt/data-access/meteo-oceanographic-forecasts/) - Official oceanographic and maritime forecast access. 🇵🇹

## Ports and marinas
- [APRAM](https://apram.pt/) - Official ports authority for Madeira. 🇵🇹
- [Portos dos Açores](https://portosdosacores.pt/) - Official Azores ports authority. 🇵🇹

## Weather
- [IPMA maritime bulletins](https://www.ipma.pt/pt/maritima/boletins/) - Best official forecast baseline in this wave for Portuguese coastal and offshore planning. 🇵🇹
- [Portuguese Hydrographic Institute meteo-oceanographic forecasts](https://www.hidrografico.pt/en/generic-pages/dt/dcdt/data-access/meteo-oceanographic-forecasts/) - Useful official follow-up for Atlantic conditions and oceanographic context. 🇵🇹

## Rules and paperwork
- Check the relevant port authority before arrival, especially if your route includes Madeira or the Azores.
- Confirm entry and clearance requirements for your flag, crew, and onward route before departure.
